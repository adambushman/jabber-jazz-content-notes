Tier 4

Jalen Hood-Schifino, 4 spots ahead of industry:

  • I buy the defense
  • Midrange capable like he is in the PnR can be a major weapon in the playoffs

Dariq Whitehead, 11 spots ahead of industry:

  • There’s a lot of self-creation masked at Duke
  • Injuries hid a lot of his athleticism

Maxwell Lewis, 9 spots ahead of industry:

  • He’s very fluid, deceptively quick first step
  • Has a foundational skill set in everything
  • His recruitment and path through Pepperdine was weird

GG Jackson, 12 spots ahead of industry, 5 spots lower than previous:

  • Rumors of poor interviews, workouts, etc.
    • Some appears valid, others appear as smoke screens
  • I just buy the package and am willing to take the big swing
  • Would absolutely take him at #16; at #28 I’d be pissed if Utah didn’t grab him

Leonard Miller, 4 spots ahead of industry, 8 spots ahead of previous:

  • Watched more of the high school tape; demonstrated a lot of initiation, passing
  • Effort and diversity of his game shown at Ignite was impressive

Bilal Coulibaly, 7 spots behind industry

  • Physical displays are impressive
    • Official measurements
    • Crazy fluid and confident
    • Thinner than anticipated
  • More raw than most admit; closer to Rayan Rupert than like Maxwell Lewis or Leonard Miller skill-wise right now
  • Don’t think there’s enough due hesitancy over that he’s played with a prospective Hall of Famer in Wembanyama and likely won’t with his new team

Gradey Dick, 9 spots behind industry

  • The lack of self-creation potential is unfortunate
  • Handle, wiggle are missing
  • Lack of defense is overrated

Dereck Lively II, 10 spots behind industry

  • I think there’s a lot of forcing Lively to DAL, NOP
  • Like Lively a ton but the C position isn’t ideal to invest in
    • They hit more often than G/F and are generally less valuable
  • Even in a redraft, given what we know about Kessler, he’s only going around 10

Nick Smith Jr., 8 spots behind industry

  • Didn’t like how he was always sped up
  • I think his floater style in the midrange and wanting to be ball dominant is a tough way to live in the NBA
